Dr. Alejandra Yep is a microbiology professor at Cal Poly.  She was born in Argentina and completed a BS in Molecular Biology and a PhD in Biological Chemistry at the University of Buenos Aires.  Her lab focuses on bacteria that cause urinary tract infections (UTIs), and she cares passionately about science communication and making STEM welcoming for all.

Dr. Jasmine Nation is a professor of science education who is passionate about university-community partnerships that benefit undergraduates and elementary learners. She focuses on science education for multilingual students of color, place-based environmental education for teachers, and creative maker and engineering design projects.

Dr. Perla Ramos Carranza currently works as Frost Postdoctoral Fellow for the Liberal Studies and Biological Sciences departments at Cal Poly. She received her PhD and M.A. in Education from UC Irvine and her B.A. in Psychology and B.S. in Human Development from UC Davis. She is committed to advancing the learning opportunities of students from marginalized communities in culturally meaningful ways.

Isiah Rivera is a Liberal Studies major with a concentration in Teaching English to Speakers of Other Languages. As an aspiring educator, he believes that every student deserves the opportunity to reach their greatest potential in a supportive and inclusive learning environment. He is passionate about helping students embrace their identities, express their ideas, and grow both academically and personally.
